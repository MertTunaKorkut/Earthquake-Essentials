4. CORE MECHANICS & GAMEPLAY FLOW
The game follows a customizable, cartoon-style protagonist navigating through a multi-phase earthquake scenario. 
4.1. Global Scoring System
•	Positive Selection: Every correct action or choice awards the player +100 points.
•	Negative Selection: Every incorrect choice penalizes the player with -50 points.
4.2. Level 1: Pre-Earthquake – Emergency Bag Preparation
•	Context: The scene is set in an ordinary room environment with an empty emergency backpack placed at the center of the screen. 
•	Core Mechanic: Drag & Drop. Items appear around the screen; the player must drag essential survival items into the bag that is in the middle. 
•	Timer: No time limit. This allows players to explore options and think critically without performance stress. 
•	Feedback & Loop: Correct items trigger positive visual/audio chimes and add points. Incorrect items trigger a humorous animation where the backpack "spits out" or rejects the item. 
•	Win Condition: The level successfully concludes once all predetermined essential items are inside the bag. The UI then transitions to show the level score summary. 
4.3. Level 2: During the Earthquake – Survival & Reflexes
•	Context: Immediately following Level 1, the scene shifts to an active earthquake. The screen shakes, objects move unpredictably, and emergency urgency increases. 
•	Core Mechanic: Point & Click. The player must evaluate the environment and click on safe hiding spots. 
•	Timer: Strict 30-second time limit per room to simulate real-time survival reflexes under pressure. 
•	Feedback & Loop: Selecting a safe zone prompts the character to perform a successful "drop, cover, and hold on" maneuver (+100 points). Selecting a hazardous zone triggers an exaggerated, comical cartoon accident (-50 points). 
•	Win Condition: The player must navigate and survive through 3 consecutive rooms (Environments) to complete the level and win the game.

5.2. Level 2 Room Matrix
The protagonist transitions automatically from Room 1 to Room 3. Each room contains exactly 2 safe zones and 2 hazardous spots. 
Room 1: Living Room (Salon)
•	Safe Zone 1: Under a sturdy wooden coffee table (Drop, Cover, Hold On). 
•	Safe Zone 2: Directly next to a heavy, solid sofa (Brace position / Life triangle).
•	Hazard Zone 1: In front of the large glass window (Shattering glass danger). 
•	Hazard Zone 2: Directly beneath the heavy, swinging ceiling chandelier. 
Room 2: Kitchen (Mutfak) 
•	Safe Zone 1: Under the heavy-duty kitchen dining table.
•	Safe Zone 2: Crouched tightly next to the lower, reinforced base kitchen counter.
•	Hazard Zone 1: In front of the tall, unsecured refrigerator (Toppling hazard). 
•	Hazard Zone 2: Directly next to the stove and upper glass spice cabinets.
Room 3: Bedroom (Yatak Odası) 
•	Safe Zone 1: Next to the bed, using a thick pillow to shield the head.
•	Safe Zone 2: Tucked safely underneath the robust wooden study desk. 
•	Hazard Zone 1: Directly in front of the bedroom window.
•	Hazard Zone 2: Right next to a tall, unanchored clothing wardrobe.
